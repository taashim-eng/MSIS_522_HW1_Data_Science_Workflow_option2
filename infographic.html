🚨 The Problem
⚠️
The Credit Invisibility Crisis
1.4 billion adults worldwide lack access to formal credit. Traditional scoring relies on credit-bureau history — excluding those with no prior loans, thin files, or unbanked status. This creates a vicious cycle: no credit → no score → no access.
📊
Data Blindness
Traditional models use only 5-10 features from FICO/credit bureaus, ignoring rich behavioral signals
🔒
Black-Box Risk
ML models can amplify existing biases in lending decisions — explainability is not optional
📈 Why Alt-Credit Scoring Matters

🎯 Project Definition
Build an end-to-end explainable credit scoring pipeline demonstrating the complete data science workflow: from data acquisition through model comparison to interactive deployment with fairness auditing.
1
Research & Data Acquisition
Identify, evaluate, and download 6+ credit datasets; document provenance and licensing
2
Exploratory Data Analysis
Distributions, correlations, class balance, fairness subgroup breakdowns
3
Predictive Modeling (5 Models)
Logistic Regression → Decision Tree → Random Forest → XGBoost → MLP Neural Network
4
Explainability & Fairness
SHAP analysis (global + local), disparate impact, equalized odds across protected groups
5
Interactive Deployment
5-tab Streamlit app with live predictions, SHAP waterfall, and ethics dashboard
✅ Desired Outcomes
🏆
Best Performing Model
Identify which of 5 models (LogReg→MLP) achieves highest AUC-ROC on default prediction. Demonstrate that ensemble/boosting methods outperform simple baselines.
🔍
Transparent Decisions
SHAP-driven explanations for every prediction — answering "WHY was this applicant denied?" at both global feature-importance and individual decision levels.
⚖️
Fair & Auditable AI
Quantified disparate impact ratios and equalized odds across gender, age, education, and marital status. Actionable mitigation strategies documented.

🏆 Finding 1: Dataset Selection Results
Primary Choice: Taiwan Credit Card Default
The optimal balance of size (30K rows), cleanliness (0 missing), and fairness features (SEX, AGE, EDUCATION, MARRIAGE). Published academic benchmark with clear binary target.
30K
Clean Rows
Why Taiwan Credit wins over all 6 candidates:
✅
Right size — Large enough for GridSearchCV + MLP, small enough for fast iteration
✅
Zero missing values — Skip imputation, focus on modeling
✅
4 protected attributes — Full fairness analysis possible (SEX, AGE, EDUCATION, MARRIAGE)
✅
6-month payment history — Rich temporal features for SHAP interpretation
✅
22% default rate — Natural imbalance motivates SMOTE, not so severe as to collapse metrics

💡 What This Means for the Audience
For Lenders & Financial Institutions
Move from opaque FICO-only decisions to multi-model, explainable scoring. Every denial comes with a clear reason — reducing regulatory risk and improving customer trust. Fairness audits prevent discriminatory lending before it reaches production.
For Borrowers & Underserved Communities
Alternative data (payment history, utility patterns) replaces missing credit-bureau records. Transparent decisions empower applicants — they know exactly which behavior to improve for better scores. Fairness checks ensure equal treatment across demographics.
For Data Scientists & Regulators
A reproducible template for responsible ML in finance: data audit → model comparison → SHAP explainability → fairness metrics → interactive deployment. Demonstrates that accuracy and fairness are not mutually exclusive.
The Bottom Line: Explainability ≠ Weakness
The best credit scoring model isn't just the most accurate — it's the one stakeholders trust, understand, and can audit. This project proves that ensemble models can be both high-performing and transparently fair.
